Bank Switch Bible

Up to £500 · 8 steps · 15 min

The easiest free money in the UK. Banks pay you £150-£500 just to move your account. Here is exactly how to do it safely and stack multiple switches.

1
Check eligibility. You need to be a new customer. Most banks require you have not held an account with them in the last 12 months.
2
Open the new account. Use the referral link in the Offers tab. Takes 5-10 minutes. Have your ID ready.
3
Start the CASS switch. CASS moves everything automatically — direct debits, standing orders, salary. Takes 7 working days. Important: do not switch your main everyday account first. Start with a secondary account.
4
Pay in the required amount. Most switches need £1,000-£1,500 within 30 days. Send it from a different account, wait at least 1 day, then send it back. Never use money you cannot spare — this is just a temporary movement.
5
Add direct debits if required. Some banks need 2 active direct debits. Use a direct debit service (see the Community tab for our recommended one) to set up cheap DDs quickly without using real subscriptions.
6
Wait for the payout. Usually 30-60 days. Add it to Pending Payouts in this app so you never forget.
7
Stack multiple switches. Once paid, switch again. Lloyds → NatWest → Santander = £500+ in 3 months. Check Offers for all current deals.
8
Pro tip. Use a direct debit service to create cheap DDs quickly. Never miss a payout — track every switch in this app.

Supermarket Stacking

Up to 8% back · 4 steps · 5 min setup

Six rules that actually work. Follow these and you will save money reliably every week without the hassle of tracking issues.

1
Start with a cashback debit card. Get a card that pays cashback automatically on most purchases — like the Chase card (1% back) or Krak card. Just use it instead of your regular card. No apps to remember, no links to click.
2
Gift cards are more reliable than cashback sites. Apps like Jam Doughnut (code 8TGF) let you buy gift cards and get instant cashback. More reliable than traditional cashback sites — fewer tracking issues and you get paid immediately.
3
Start fresh for each cashback purchase. Clear your browser history or use a private window. Accept cookies. Do not use coupon extensions like Honey — they interfere with cashback tracking. Click the cashback link and go straight to checkout.
4
Test with a small purchase first. Before making a big purchase through a cashback site, buy something small like a £5 gift card to confirm the tracking works on your device and browser.
5
Take guaranteed discounts over cashback promises. Always choose a definite discount or coupon code over potential cashback. Some cashback sites will not pay if you used a discount code — a guaranteed saving now beats a promised payment later.
6
Withdraw your earnings regularly. Do not leave money sitting in cashback accounts. Take it out as soon as you hit the minimum withdrawal amount. Money in your account is real — money in theirs is not.

Get free shares just for depositing £1-£50. Genuinely low effort — deposit a small amount, get a random free share, withdraw when the hold period ends.

Then Robinhood. Same deal — deposit £1, get a free share. Stack both in the same week.
3

Move to bigger bonuses. Webull (£50 for £100 deposit), InvestEngine (up to £50), Lightyear (free shares for £50). All in Offers tab.

2
Week 2 — Bank switch 1. Open Lloyds, start CASS switch, deposit £1,000. Payout: £250 in about 30 days.
3
Week 3 — Bank switch 2. Open NatWest or Santander while waiting for Lloyds. Switches run in parallel.
4

Month 2 — Supermarket stacking. Set up Jam Doughnut and Airtime. Buy gift cards before every shop. Saves £30-£50 per month ongoing.
